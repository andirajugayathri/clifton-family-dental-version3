How Does Teeth Whitening Work?

The first step of teeth whitening is professional consultation. The dentist will assess the condition of your teeth and gums to ensure this treatment is suitable for you. There's no need to be too concerned about this process, most healthy adult patients can safely receive teeth whitening treatment.

The treatment process starts with protecting the gums and lips so that only the teeth are exposed during the procedure. Next, the dentist applies a bleaching gel over the teeth and uses a special laser device to speed up the whitening process. The procedure is performed in rounds so that your teeth can be whitened to your desired shade. The results are beautifully white teeth!

Frequently Asked Questions

Professional teeth whitening performed by a qualified dental professional is a safe and effective treatment for healthy adult patients. It is not, however, suitable for everyone. Patients who are pregnant and those with severe tooth sensitivity and certain health conditions are advised not to receive the treatment. Professional consultation is necessary to determine your suitability for this treatment and ensure it will be safe and effective for you. This is why dentists advise against people using DIY teeth bleaching methods, they can be dangerous without professional supervision.

While our professional teeth whitening in Clifton Beach is suitable for a lot of adult patients, our dentists advise it is not suitable for patients who are pregnant or breastfeeding. If you are pregnant and interested in teeth whitening, contact our team to discuss how soon after delivery is recommended before you can book an appointment.

It is completely natural for teeth to yellow, stain or discolour over time even for people who have received teeth whitening treatment. While it is not a permanent cosmetic treatment, the results from teeth whitening can last for a few years afterwards. To maintain professional teeth whitening results for as long as possible, patients should avoid tooth-staining food, drinks and habits (like smoking), practice good dental hygiene like daily brushing and flossing and keep up with regular dental check-ups.
