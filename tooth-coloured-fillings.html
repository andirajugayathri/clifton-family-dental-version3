Tooth-Coloured Fillings

Tooth Fillings are a very common treatment for patients of all ages who have cavities in their teeth. They are used to seal the hole (cavity), and protect and restore the tooth's shape. Tooth coloured fillings are regular dental fillings made from composite resin. They are known as tooth coloured because of their colour which can be matched to the colour of your teeth.

Signs You May Need A Tooth Filling

Small cavities and decay may not always be obvious to a patient but can be spotted by a dentist during a check-up. Some signs that you have a dental cavity that may need a filling are:

  • Toothache
  • Tooth pain or sensitivity to cool or hot temperatures
  • Visible holes in a tooth
  • Dark stains or discolouration on a tooth's surface
  • Pain or sensitivity when biting down

Frequently Asked Questions

Tooth-colored fillings made from composite resin are very durable. They typically last 10 to 12 years before needing to be replaced by a dentist.

Most patients find getting tooth fillings to be relatively painless, especially considering the pain and sensitivity of having a cavity or tooth decay. After getting a tooth filling, these symptoms generally go away within a day or two. If you are anxious or feel any sort of discomfort during your appointment, sedation options are available to help you feel more at ease during the treatment.

In Australia, tooth-colored fillings made from composite resin are the most common choice by dentists. Silver fillings made from amalgam were widely used in the past, however, they are not preferable today as their appearance is less discrete and the metal contains mercury which may be harmful to people with certain health conditions.
